INGREDIENTS

—100g of levain (that is your mature starter)

—376-378g of water (room temp, filtered)

—500g of flour (I usually do 375 or 400 white bread flour, remainder whole wheat flour, but do whatever you got)

—13g of salt

 

 

THE NIGHT BEFORE

  1. Toss out half on your starter - or make some crackers with it https://www.loveandoliveoil.com/2019/03/sourdough-crackers-with-olive-oil-herbs.html
  2. Feed it 70 grams water + 70 grams flour (which you've mixed into a little doughy glob in another ball)
  3. Leave it out overnight, somewhere that's not too cold
  4. If you can, try to start the mixing process the next day when your starter looks the most bubbly.

 

 

MIXING

  1. Mix 100g of levain (that is your mature starter) with the room temp filtered 376-378g water. You get a milky looking water from that.

  2. In a separate bowl, you’ll want 500g of flour (white bread flour or a mix if you have it)

  3. Then pour the water-starter mixture into the dry flour and mix until the flour is fully hydrated (no dry crumbles on the bottom of the bowl)

  4. Once you have mixed everything together. Let it sit in the bowl for 30min to an hour. I usually do 45 min.

  5. After 45 min, add in 13g of salt and fold into the dough.

     

    This is all your ingredients

     

SHAPING

Then, you will work the dough (fold over and over in the bowl) every 30 min for about 3 hours, basically you're looking for the dough to hold together into a ball. I like to think of it as stretching the dough over istelf like a Popple! Remember Popples!

You're trying to really stretch out the gluten here. Not tear it apart.

 

Let's look at my white and wheat flour mixes side by side, both after 3 folds ( half way there). As you can see, the wheat one looked a bit more gelled. This one was 250g einkorn wheat and 250g sprouted wheat flour

 

The white flour version was still pretty wet when I plopped it into my bowl (see below) but it still worked! There's lots to be read about dough hydration, and temperature — but I haven't done that yet...

SHAPING THE BOULE

So now that you've folded it 6 times, it should not really be sticky anymore. It's time to get your bread ready for an overnight in the fridge

 

  1. You’ll want to lightly flour a surface and start the the boule shaping process

  2. Basically, you’ll fold it in on itself and then turn over so the seams are down on the floured counter/surface. This is a good guide: https://www.theperfectloaf.com/guides/shaping-a-boule/

  3. Once you have all the seams down you wanna twist your bread on the surface. Think of it as if there was Saran Wrap around your bread and you want to twist it taught at the bottom, making your bread smooth to the touch.

  4. And then let it sit on the counter for 30-40 min. This is called the bench rest. This stage is really important. It lets all the glutens relax so it’s not like a tight muscle. (I have forgotten to do this both times!)

  5. After the bench rest, use your hands or your bench scraper to scoop under the bread and place it round side down into a heavily floured proofing basket or bowl.

  6. If you want, you can pull the bread even tighter at this point. This is what he’s talking about in the “pinching a weak dough” section in that link above.

  7. Then, cover the bowl/basket with a cloth + plate/cover and put in your refrigerator overnight

     

 

BAKING

  1. Preheat your oven to 500 degrees.
  2. Put a cast iron with a top in there.

SHAPING THE LOAF

  1. I'm still working on this part, but there is a good guide here:8. Bake – Next Morning: Preheat oven at 8:30 a.m., Bake at 9:30 a.m. https://www.theperfectloaf.com/beginners-sourdough-bread/
  2. For me, I turn out the bread out onto a parchment paper a bit larger than my Dutch oven a and try to shape it into an oval. (My Dutch oven is not round)

  1. Then, slice it down the center with an Exacto-Knife. This is to help it rise predictably in the over, I am told. Some people do some pretty cool designs...
  2. Check that your oven is up to 500 degrees.
  3. If the oven is ready, remove the dutch oven and carefully lower in your bread (cradleing it in the parchment paper) into it withouth burning yourself. Cover and reuturn ever-so-carefully to the oven.

 

 

BAKING CONT.

  1. Now that it's in the oven. Set a timer for 20 minutes.

  2. After 20 minutes, remove the lid and turn down the heat to 450 degrees.

  3. Bake uncovered fro 30-40 minutes, or until the surface is deeply browned

  4. Once you're there take it out of the oven BUT DO NOT SLICE IT

 

COOLING

  1. Remove from the oven, I use the parchment paper, but others use tongs

  2. Let rest on your counter for at least an hour. The post-bake rest keeps your bread moist. If you cut too early, the bread will let out steam and lose its hydration.

     

AND NOW THE BEST STEP

  1. Cut
  2. Eat
